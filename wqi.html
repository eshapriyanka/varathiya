Water Quality Index

WQI (Water Quality Index) is a numerical value representing overall water quality by combining multiple water parameters.
It is widely used for environmental monitoring, public awareness, and water resource management.
The Water Quality Index (WQI) simplifies the assessment of water quality by combining parameters like pH, dissolved oxygen, biological oxygen demand (BOD), and others into a single score. This score, ranging from 0 to 100, helps classify water quality from excellent to very poor, making it easy to understand the health of water bodies.
WQI is useful for environmental monitoring, water resource management, and raising public awareness about pollution. It provides essential data for decision-making in pollution control, drinking water safety, and ecosystem protection, aiding government bodies and researchers in maintaining water quality.

WQI = ITEMP * (IBOD + ITSS + IDO + ICOND)
